# Vea Bridge

Vea is the cross-chain messaging bridge that enables Kleros to serve Arbitrable contracts on foreign chains while KlerosCore runs on Arbitrum.

## Design

Vea uses an **optimistic verification** model:

1. A **bridger** (claimer) posts a message batch claim on the receiving chain with a deposit
2. After a challenge period, if unchallenged, the claim is accepted and messages are relayed
3. If challenged, the dispute is resolved (using Kleros itself as backstop)

## Happy Path

<Steps>
  <Step title="Messages Sent">
    Messages accumulate on the sending chain (e.g., Arbitrum).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Snapshot">
    A snapshot of the message batch is taken.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Claim">
    A bridger posts the claim on the receiving chain with a deposit.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Challenge Period">
    Anyone can challenge if the claim is incorrect.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Execution">
    If unchallenged, messages are relayed to their recipients.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Unhappy Path

If a claim is challenged:
1. The challenger posts a counter-deposit
2. The dispute is resolved using the native bridge as the source of truth (slower but trustless)
3. The dishonest party loses their deposit

## Supported Routes

| From | To | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Arbitrum → Ethereum | Ethereum → Arbitrum | Active |
| Arbitrum → Gnosis | Gnosis → Arbitrum | Active |
